# Qantas Points & Amex MR: Premium Flight Redemption Reference

A practical reference for using Qantas Frequent Flyer points and Australian Amex Membership Rewards to book premium economy and business class flights — with a focus on Melbourne departures and the Australia–UK route.

This document provides examples, shortcuts, and pointers rather than exhaustive tables. For precise per-route calculations, use the [Qantas Points Calculator](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/calculators.html) or the official points tables linked throughout.

---

## 1. Earning: Amex Membership Rewards → Qantas Points

### Transfer Rates (Australia, as of June 2026)

Amex MR points in Australia transfer to Qantas FF at different rates depending on your card.

**Direct transfer (Amex Platinum & Centurion charge cards only):**
- 2 Amex MR points → 1 Qantas point (2:1 ratio)
- Transfer is instant; no minimum

**Indirect route (all other Amex cards):**
- Transfer MR to Woolworths Everyday Rewards at 5:4 (i.e. 5 MR = 4 Everyday Rewards)
- Transfer Everyday Rewards to Qantas FF at 2:1
- Net effective rate: 2.5 MR per 1 Qantas point

The indirect route adds complexity but opens Qantas transfers to a broader range of cards. Watch for periodic bonus transfer promotions on the MR → Everyday Rewards leg (e.g. a 15% bonus ran until mid-June 2026).

> **Important:** From 15 December 2025, Amex devalued transfers to several airline partners. Qantas via direct transfer (Platinum/Centurion) was **not** devalued and remains at 2:1. The indirect Everyday Rewards route is newer and was introduced as part of those changes.

## 2. Reward Types at a Glance

Qantas offers three ways to use points on flights:

### Classic Flight Rewards ⬥ Red ribbon on qantas.com
- **Fixed** points cost year-round (zone-based, not fare-based)
- Available on Qantas, Jetstar, and 30+ partner airlines
- Availability is **limited** and capacity-controlled
- Best value per point; requires flexibility on dates
- [Classic Flight Rewards overview](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-flight-rewards.html)
- [Full points tables](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-flight-rewards/tables.html)

### Classic Plus Flight Rewards ⬥ Blue ribbon on qantas.com
- Points cost **fluctuates** with cash fare prices
- Qantas-operated flights only (not partners)
- Much more widely available than Classic
- Changes to bookings incur a cash fee
- [Classic Plus overview](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-plus-flight-rewards.html)

### Points + Pay
- Use any combination of points and cash on any seat for sale
- Available on QF, JQ, and selected partners
- No fixed rate; generally the lowest-value use of points
- [Points + Pay overview](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/points-plus-pay.html)

### Classic Upgrade Rewards
- Use points to upgrade a confirmed paid ticket to the next cabin
- Available on Qantas-operated (QF-numbered) flights only
- Requests processed based on status tier (Platinum One > Platinum > Gold/Silver/Bronze)
- See [§5 Upgrade Rewards](#5-upgrade-rewards) below
- [Classic Upgrade Rewards overview](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-upgrade-rewards.html)

## 3. Classic Flight Reward Points: Representative Examples

Points are calculated per-passenger, per-direction (one-way), based on **flight distance in miles** mapped to one of 10 zones. The relevant zone is determined by the total itinerary distance, not the individual legs.

The tables below show **one-way points required (excluding taxes, fees and carrier charges)**, using **Current rates effective from 5 August 2025**.

There are separate (slightly higher) rates for partner airlines vs Qantas/AA/Jetstar/Fiji. All examples below show both where relevant.

**Official tables:** [qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-flight-rewards/tables.html](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-flight-rewards/tables.html)

**Official calculator:** [qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/calculators.html](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/calculators.html)

### Domestic examples (MEL base)

| Route | Zone | Distance | Economy | Business |
|-------|------|----------|---------|---------|
| MEL–SYD | 1 | ~470 mi | 9,200 | 19,300 |
| MEL–BNE | 2 | ~910 mi | 13,800 | 29,000 |
| MEL–ADL | 1 | ~370 mi | 9,200 | 19,300 |
| MEL–PER | 3 | ~1,700 mi | 20,700 | 43,600 |

### Short-haul international examples (MEL base)

| Route | Zone | Airline type | Economy | Prem Eco | Business |
|-------|------|-------------|---------|----------|---------|
| MEL–SIN | 5 | Qantas/AA | 29,000 | 61,600 | 82,100 |
| MEL–SIN | 5 | Partner (BA, CX, etc.) | 34,700 | 70,800 | 90,000 |
| MEL–NRT (Tokyo) | 6 | Qantas/AA | 36,200 | 73,800 | 98,400 |
| MEL–NRT | 6 | Partner (JAL, etc.) | 43,500 | 85,000 | 108,000 |
| MEL–HKG | 5 | Partner (Cathay) | 34,700 | 70,800 | 90,000 |
| MEL–DXB | 8 | Emirates | 48,200 | — | 130,100 |

*Emirates has its own table (separate from the Partner table) effective 31 March 2026.*

### Long-haul examples: Australia–UK/Europe (one-way)

MEL–LHR and SYD–LHR are ~10,500 miles, placing them in **Zone 10** (9,601–15,000 miles).

| Route | Airline / Via | Economy | Prem Eco | Business | First |
|-------|--------------|---------|----------|---------|-------|
| MEL–LHR | Qantas (via SIN or PER) | 63,500 | 124,700 | **166,300** | 249,400 |
| MEL–LHR | Partner (BA, CX, QR, JAL, MAS, Finnair…) | 76,100 | 143,500 | **182,900** | 261,600 |
| MEL–LHR | Emirates (via DXB) | 69,900 | 164,700 | **182,900** | 299,300 |

> ⚡ **Perth advantage:** PER–LHR (Qantas non-stop Dreamliner) is ~9,009 miles, landing in **Zone 9** — one zone cheaper. Qantas Zone 9: Economy 58,900 / Prem Eco 113,900 / Business **151,800**. This makes a MEL–PER connection + PER–LHR reward potentially worthwhile if PER–LHR reward space is available.

**Return trip cost:**
The tables above are per direction. A return MEL–LHR–MEL in Business on partner airlines would be 182,900 × 2 = **365,800 points** if booked as two one-way Classic Rewards.

Alternatively, the [oneworld Classic Reward](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-flight-rewards.html) covers round trips on 2+ oneworld airlines for a flat fee. Zone 10 return (19,201–35,000 miles) Business = **365,800 points** — effectively the same cost, but allows stopovers and routing flexibility (see §7 for details).

### American Express: effective point cost to fly Business MEL–LHR

| Card type | MR needed per Qantas pt | MR to buy 182,900 QF pts |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Amex Platinum/Centurion (direct, 2:1) | 2 | ~365,800 MR |
| Other Amex (indirect via Everyday Rewards, 2.5:1) | 2.5 | ~457,250 MR |

## 4. Emirates: Special Considerations

Emirates has its own points table (effective 31 March 2026), distinct from the Partner table. Key differences:

- **Economy is cheaper** than Partner table: Zone 10 Economy = 69,900 vs 76,100 (Partner)
- **Business matches** Partner table: Zone 10 Business = 182,900 (same)
- **High carrier charges**: Emirates is notorious for high taxes/fees (~AU$1,100+ each way to London), significantly more than Asian-hub partners (~AU$460–600)
- **Wide availability**: Emirates typically releases more reward seats than other partners

From the Flight Reward Finder (verified June 2026, MEL/SYD → LHR):
- Economy via Emirates (DXB): **69,900 pts + ~AU$465**
- Business via partner (CX, QR etc.): **182,900 pts + ~AU$594**

## 5. Upgrade Rewards

Classic Upgrade Rewards let you use points to upgrade a **paid, confirmed ticket** one cabin class on Qantas-operated (QF-coded) flights. Partner airline flights are not eligible.

**Official upgrade tables:** [qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-upgrade-rewards/tables.html](https://www.qantas.com/au/en/frequent-flyer/use-points/classic-upgrade-rewards/tables.html)

### Upgrade points required: international examples (Zone 10, e.g. MEL–LHR)

*Current rates from 5 August 2025:*

| Upgrading from | To Business | To First |
|---------------|-------------|---------|
| Economy Classic Flight Reward | 162,100 | — |
| Economy (paid, standard classes G/K/L/M/S/V) | 157,000 | — |
| Flexible Economy (Y/B/H class paid) | **86,300** | — |
| Premium Economy Classic Reward | 111,100 | — |
| Premium Economy (paid standard) | 86,300 | — |
| Business Classic Reward | — | not eligible |
| Business (paid, Discount) | — | 98,000 |
| Business (paid, standard) | — | **88,200** |
| Business (paid, Flexible) | — | 78,500 |

> **Value note:** Upgrading from an Economy Classic Reward (63,500 pts) costs 162,100 upgrade points, totalling 225,600 pts — **more** than buying Business directly (166,300 pts on Qantas). The upgrade path only makes financial sense if you hold a paid Economy ticket, particularly a **Flexible Economy** (86,300 pts upgrade) where the savings vs buying Business outright can be significant.

### How upgrades are processed

Requests are reviewed based on membership tier:

| Tier | International upgrade window |
|------|----------------------------|
| Platinum One | Within 7 days of departure |
| Platinum | Within 3 days of departure |
| Gold, Silver, Bronze | Within 24 hours of departure |

Points are only deducted **when the upgrade is confirmed**, not when you request it. You can request upgrades up to 10 hours before departure on international flights.

## 6. Technical: Searching for Classic Flight Rewards

### 6a. Flight Reward Finder (`flightrewardfinder.qantas.com`)

Launched in 2025/26, this is Qantas's dedicated tool for finding international Classic Flight Rewards across Qantas, Jetstar, and 30 partner airlines. It shows live availability by date, with colour-coded calendar views (red = Economy, pink = Premium Economy, black = Business, light blue = First).

> 🔓 **No login required** to browse availability and search routes.

> **Key characteristic:** This is a discovery tool only — you cannot book directly from it. Once you identify a flight, you click through to complete the booking on qantas.com (login required there).

#### Base URL

```
https://flightrewardfinder.qantas.com/
```

#### URL Parameters

| Parameter | Description | Example values |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| `o` | Origin (IATA airport code) | `MEL`, `SYD`, `BNE`, `PER` |
| `d` | Destination (IATA airport code) | `LHR`, `LGW`, `STN`, `CDG`, `HND` |
| `c` | Cabin filter | `Economy`, `Premium Economy`, `Business`, `First` |
| `dr` | Date range (ISO dates with `I` separator) | `2026-10-01I2026-10-31` |
| `pg` | Page number | `1` |

Omitting `c` shows all cabins. Omitting `dr` shows all available dates.

**Important:** IATA city codes (like `LON`) are **not** supported — use airport codes (`LHR`, `LGW`, `STN`) to target specific airports. The tool accepts one destination airport at a time.

#### Worked examples

*URL structure verified June 2026.*

**Business class, MEL → LHR, any date:**
```
https://flightrewardfinder.qantas.com/?pg=1&o=MEL&d=LHR&c=Business
```

**All cabins, MEL → LHR, October 2026:**
```
https://flightrewardfinder.qantas.com/?pg=1&o=MEL&d=LHR&dr=2026-10-01I2026-10-31
```

**Business class, MEL → LHR, specific week:**
```
https://flightrewardfinder.qantas.com/?pg=1&o=MEL&d=LHR&c=Business&dr=2026-10-27I2026-11-03
```

**Premium Economy, MEL → NRT (Tokyo), January 2027:**
```
https://flightrewardfinder.qantas.com/?pg=1&o=MEL&d=NRT&c=Premium%20Economy&dr=2027-01-01I2027-01-31
```

#### Notes and quirks

- The calendar on the date picker shows colour-coded dots for each cabin class. Clicking a month in the left-hand list selects the full calendar month as the date range.
- Results show live availability and are checked against real inventory. Availability shown is not guaranteed until booked on qantas.com.
- The `c` parameter values must match exactly (case-sensitive, space-sensitive): `Economy`, `Premium Economy`, `Business`, `First`. Using `Business Class` or `business` will likely default to all cabins.
- Partner airline results (e.g. Qatar, Cathay, BA, JAL) appear alongside Qantas own flights. Results include the airline logo, routing, duration, stops, and points required per cabin.
- The tool refreshes availability in the background; "Last checked" timestamps are shown per row.
- A warning banner was shown in June 2026: *"Due to the ongoing operational impacts of events in the Middle East, reward seat availability across select regions is now limited on both Qantas and our airline partners."*

### 6b. qantas.com Booking Search

> 🔒 **Login required** to see Classic Flight Reward pricing. Without a logged-in Frequent Flyer account, only paid cash fares display — the rewards pricing is hidden entirely.

For making the actual reward booking (or checking seat availability while logged in), use the standard Qantas search page.

> ⚠️ **No deep-link URL exists (verified June 2026).** Unlike the Flight Reward Finder, the Qantas booking search **does not support pre-filled deep-link URLs**. URL parameters (`?origin=MEL&destination=LHR&bookingType=classicAward` etc.) are accepted by the page but do **not** pre-populate the form fields — the form loads blank regardless of what parameters are in the URL. You must enter the search criteria manually.

#### Entry point (fill manually)

```
https://www.qantas.com/en-au/book/flights
```

1. Enter departure and arrival airports
2. Select travel dates (the calendar shows Classic / Classic Plus availability colour-coded)
3. Toggle **Rewards** on (required to see Classic Flight Reward pricing)
4. Click **Search flights**

#### What happens after clicking Search

The system redirects to a session-specific URL on `book.qantas.com`:

```
https://book.qantas.com/qf-booking/dyn/air/booking/FFPP?SITE=QFQFQFBI&LANGUAGE=GB&TAB_ID=<session-token>
```

The `TAB_ID` is a one-time session token — it cannot be constructed, shared, or reused. The `book.qantas.com` results page is not bookmarkable.

#### Reading results

- The date strip at the top shows the cheapest available reward type for nearby dates (Classic in red, Classic Plus in blue)
- Results show **Economy Reward** and **Business Reward** sections (cabin is pre-filtered based on your search)
- Use the **Rewards** tab (vs "All Flights") to filter to points-only results
- Classic Rewards show with a **red ribbon**; Classic Plus with a **blue ribbon**

#### Notes

- The calendar on the date picker shows coloured dots for available reward types — useful for spotting Classic availability windows before committing to a date.
- Verified June 2026: MEL → LHR, October 2026, Economy with Rewards on showed Classic at **63,500 pts + ~AU$258** and Classic Plus at **75,400 pts + ~AU$247**.

## 7. Australia → UK: Tips and Tactics

> **Note:** This section covers Australia-origin redemptions using Qantas points. For UK-origin redemptions on the same route using BA Executive Club Avios (including the Amex Companion Voucher), see the [BA Avios guide](./ba.md). Booking one-way in each direction through different programmes is often the best-value approach.

### Route options from Melbourne

Melbourne has no direct service to London. All routings involve at least one stop. Current viable routes for Classic Flight Rewards from MEL:

| Via | Airline | Stop | Typical duration |
|-----|---------|------|-----------------|
| Singapore (SIN) | Qantas (QF9) | 1 | ~22–23 hrs |
| Perth (PER) | Qantas (QF9) — non-stop PER–LHR | 1 (PER) | ~26 hrs |
| Dubai (DXB) | Emirates | 1 | ~22–24 hrs |
| Doha (DOH) | Qatar Airways | 1 | ~22–24 hrs |
| Hong Kong (HKG) | Cathay Pacific | 1 | ~22–25 hrs |
| Kuala Lumpur (KUL) | Malaysia Airlines | 1 | ~23–25 hrs |
| Tokyo (NRT/HND) | Japan Airlines | 1 | ~27–30 hrs |
| Helsinki (HEL) | Connect to Finnair in SIN/HKG | 2 | ~30+ hrs |
| London (LHR) | British Airways via SIN | 2 | ~26+ hrs |

### Points cost comparison for MEL–LHR Business (one-way)

| Option | Points | Fees/taxes (approx.) | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------------------|-------|
| Qantas own flights (QF9) | **166,300** | ~AU$500 | Hardest to find; status helps |
| Qatar Qsuites / Cathay / JAL | **182,900** | ~AU$500–600 | Reliably releases 2 seats per flight |
| Emirates | **182,900** | ~AU$1,100+ | Wide availability; very high carrier charges |
| Malaysia Airlines | **182,900** | ~AU$400–500 | Less-known option; good A350 product KUL–LHR |
| Finnair (via SIN) | **182,900** | **AU$0 carrier charges** | No carrier charges is unique; adds a leg |
| MEL–PER + PER–LHR (Qantas) | ~166,300+20,700 | ~AU$600 | Only works if PER–LHR Business space exists |

### Carrier charges: the hidden cost

Taxes, fees, and carrier charges are payable *in addition to* points and can significantly vary:

- **Finnair:** No carrier charges — you pay only government taxes (~AU$50–100 each way). This is a significant saving.
- **Japan Airlines:** Low carrier charges
- **Qatar Airways / Cathay Pacific:** Moderate (~AU$500–600 each way in Business)
- **Qantas:** Moderate (~AU$500 each way in Business)
- **Emirates:** High carrier charges (~AU$1,100+ each way in Business/First)
- **British Airways:** High carrier charges (avoid for LHR-departing segments if possible)

> When comparing options, always factor in the total cost (points + fees). A "cheaper" partner fare with high carrier charges may cost more in real terms than a "pricier" one with low charges.

### Booking strategies

**Book early.** Classic Flight Reward seats to the UK are released up to ~330 days in advance. The best availability for Qantas own flights and popular partners (QR, CX) is at the time of initial release. Set up alerts or check regularly.

**Status helps for Qantas own flights.** Qantas Business and First Class reward seats on QF-operated London flights are very tightly held. Gold and above members can sometimes call Qantas to request additional seats be released — success varies. For partner airlines, availability is not status-gated.

**Qatar Airways releases reliably.** Qatar has historically released two Business Class and two First Class reward seats on most Australia–Europe flights. It's consistently one of the most reliably bookable partners for premium cabin rewards.

**Consider the oneworld Classic Reward for return trips.** If you're flying return on at least two different oneworld airlines (e.g. Cathay outbound, Qatar return), you can book under the oneworld award table. At Zone 10 return (19,201–35,000 miles), the Business rate is **365,800 points** for the round trip — the same cost as two one-way Classic Rewards, but it adds flexibility to include stopovers (up to 5 cities on 2+ oneworld airlines). This makes it useful for routing through Asia or the Middle East.

**Perth routing can save points.** If you're willing to connect through Perth, PER–LHR on the Qantas Dreamliner falls in Zone 9 (151,800 pts Business) rather than Zone 10 (166,300 pts). The MEL–PER leg is cheap domestically (Zone 3, ~20,700 pts Economy or 43,600 Business). Combining MEL–PER in Economy + PER–LHR in Business can save points vs MEL–LHR Business if PER–LHR Business space is available. Use the Flight Reward Finder to check both.

**Avoid Emirates for Business if charges matter.** While Emirates has wide Business award availability, the carrier charges can be AU$700–1,100+ more per ticket than Middle Eastern or Asian competitors. This typically makes it a poor-value choice for Business despite solid availability.

**Finnair is a sleeper pick.** No carrier charges is a rare and valuable characteristic. Finnair doesn't fly to Australia, but you can book it as a connecting segment from Singapore (or Hong Kong) to Helsinki and onwards to most European cities. The total routing (MEL–SIN on Qantas, then SIN–HEL–destination on Finnair) qualifies as a Classic Flight Reward.

**Flexibility on the European end.** Arriving into LGW or STN instead of LHR can sometimes unlock more reward availability, since fewer passengers route there. Paris CDG, Amsterdam AMS, or Frankfurt FRA are also viable arrival airports for many partners.

**Use the Flight Reward Finder calendar view.** The colour-coded calendar quickly shows which months/weeks have Business class reward space (black dot). This is the fastest way to identify availability windows without clicking through individual dates.
