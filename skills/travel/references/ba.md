# British Airways Avios: Flight Redemption Reference

A practical reference for using BA Executive Club Avios — earned via the American Express® Premium Plus Card — to book premium flights from London. Covers BA's own flights, Avios group partners, and oneworld partner airlines.

This document provides examples and tactics rather than exhaustive tables. For exact per-route calculations, use [BA's award chart tool](https://www.britishairways.com/en-gb/information/avios-programmes/british-airways-executive-club/spending-avios/reward-flights) or the resources linked throughout.

### Companion Voucher

The voucher is the card's headline benefit. At £15,000 spend in a card year, you receive a **2-for-1 Avios voucher** valid on Reward Flight bookings on British Airways, Iberia, and Aer Lingus. Key details:

- **Both passengers fly**: you pay Avios for one ticket; the companion's Avios cost is waived. You pay taxes and carrier surcharges for both passengers.
- **All cabins**: valid in Economy, Premium Economy, Club World (Business), and First.
- **Solo use**: if travelling alone, you get a 50% Avios discount on a single ticket instead of a 2-for-1.
- **Premium Plus seat access**: cardholders can access additional Club World Reward seats via "Companion Voucher Fares" (CVF) — a pool of seats not otherwise bookable online — which significantly improves availability for business class redemptions.
- **Maximising value**: the voucher is most valuable used on the longest-haul business or first class route you'd fly, where the saved Avios are worth the most. Using it on a short European hop wastes the benefit.

### Accumulating Avios faster

- Spend on BA.com and BA Holidays earns 3×
- Large one-off purchases on the card during the 3-month bonus window can reach the 30,000 bonus quickly
- Avios are also earned on most BA flights flown (proportional to fare class and distance)

## 2. The Avios Group — Transfer Partners

Avios earned in one programme transfer **1:1 instantly** to any other programme in the group. This is not a devaluation or conversion — it's a direct account-to-account transfer.

| Programme | Key use case |
|-----------|-------------|
| **BA Executive Club** | BA, Iberia, Aer Lingus flights; all partner bookings via ba.com |
| **Iberia Plus** | Iberia flights at lower surcharges; Madrid hub connections |
| **Aer Lingus AerClub** | Dublin hub transatlantic routes |
| **Finnair Plus** | Finnair flights; Helsinki hub |
| **Qatar Airways Privilege Club** | Qatar flights; Doha hub; excellent long-haul availability |
| **Vueling Club** | Low-cost short-haul European routes |

**Why this matters:** Booking the same route through a different Avios programme can mean lower carrier surcharges and different seat availability. The most important example is transatlantic: booking on Iberia (transferred Avios) instead of BA can cut the cash component by hundreds of pounds. See [Section 6 (Carrier Surcharges)](#6-carrier-surcharges).

## 6. Carrier Surcharges

This is **the most important variable** in any BA Avios booking. Even with a "fixed" Avios cost, the mandatory cash component varies enormously depending on which airline operates the flight.

### BA own flights: High surcharges

BA imposes carrier surcharges on its Reward Flights (sometimes called "fuel surcharges" though they're not linked to actual fuel costs). These were significantly increased from **27 May 2026**.

*Example post-May 2026:*
- LHR → JFK → LHR, Club World (Business), return: **176,000 Avios + ~£499** cash
- LHR → LAX → LHR, Club World, return: approximately similar scale

The cash component is collected on top of Avios, not instead of it. For many travellers, the £400–£600 cash cost per round-trip erodes the value of the Avios redemption.

### American Airlines: Near-zero surcharges

When you book an AA-operated flight using Avios (booked through ba.com), AA charges **no carrier surcharge** — you pay only mandatory government taxes and a small US security fee (~$5.60 per sector).

*Example: LHR → JFK one-way on AA, Business:*
- Avios cost: ~62,000 (Zone 5 single-partner rate) vs 50,000 on BA
- Cash: ~£30–50 vs ~£250+ on BA own metal

The Avios cost is higher on the single-partner rate, but the cash saving is usually substantial enough to make AA the better option unless you specifically want to fly BA.

### Iberia: Low surcharges (transatlantic sweet spot)

Transfer Avios to **Iberia Plus** (1:1, instant) and book Iberia-operated transatlantic flights for significantly lower surcharges.

*Example: MAD → JFK one-way on Iberia, Business, off-peak:*
- **34,000 Avios + ~£115** cash

This requires connecting via Madrid, adding travel time, but for those originating in London it can be worthwhile. Book directly at [iberia.com](https://www.iberia.com/en/) using your transferred Iberia Plus Avios.

### Finnair: Low-moderate

Finnair imposes low surcharges on most routes. Useful for European hops and for connecting to Asia via Helsinki.

### Qatar Airways: Moderate

Qatar flights booked via ba.com or via Qatar Privilege Club (after Avios transfer) carry moderate surcharges. Qatar's long-haul business class (QSuites) is highly regarded.

### Summary guide

| Airline | Surcharge level | Best for |
|---------|----------------|---------|
| British Airways | **High** (especially post May 2026) | When BA route/timing can't be matched elsewhere |
| American Airlines | **Near-zero** | North Atlantic (LHR–US) |
| Iberia | **Low** | Transatlantic via MAD; transfer Avios to Iberia Plus |
| Aer Lingus | **Low-moderate** | Transatlantic via DUB |
| Finnair | **Low-moderate** | Europe, Asia via HEL |
| Qatar Airways | **Moderate** | Middle East, Asia, Africa via DOH |
| Cathay Pacific | **Moderate** | Asia, Australia via HKG |
| JAL | **Moderate** | Japan, Asia |

## 8. Companion Voucher — Complete Guide

### 8a. Finding your voucher

Once your Amex spending hits £15,000 in a card year, Amex deposits the voucher into your BA Executive Club account within 2–3 days. To confirm it's there: log into ba.com → click your name (top left) → **Membership** tab → scroll to **Member vouchers**. The voucher shows its type, issue date, and expiry.

The voucher from the **Premium Plus** card is valid for **2 years** and usable in any cabin. The voucher from the free BA Amex is valid for **1 year** and Economy-only.

### 8b. The critical "use a voucher" checkbox

> ⚠️ **You must tick "use a voucher" before searching, not just during checkout.**

When you're logged into ba.com and have an active voucher, a **"Use a Companion Voucher"** checkbox appears on the "Book a flight with Avios" search form. Ticking this before clicking "Get flights" does two things:

1. Signals to the booking engine to apply the voucher (so passenger 2 flies on your Avios)
2. **Unlocks additional Club World (Business) seats** that are not visible in standard reward searches

This second point is crucial: BA makes a separate pool of extra business class reward seats available exclusively to Premium Plus cardholders who tick the voucher box. These seats are **invisible** to anyone searching normally — including SeatSpy and Reward Flight Finder.

> **If you don't tick the box, you're not seeing all available seats.** This applies even if you're just exploring options — you should tick the box any time you're checking Business class availability for a companion voucher trip.

The checkbox **only appears when you are logged in and have an active voucher**. If the box doesn't appear, either you're not logged in or no active voucher is in the account. There is no URL shortcut that replicates this — the voucher check is session/account-based.

### 8c. Searching with the voucher

The booking flow with a companion voucher is identical to a normal reward search, with one extra step:

1. Go to **Book → Book a flight with Avios** on ba.com
2. **Tick the "Use a Companion Voucher" checkbox** (top of the form — Premium Plus cardholders see this when logged in with an active voucher)
3. Set passengers to **2 adults** (for 2-for-1) or **1 adult** (for 50% solo discount)
4. Enter route, dates, and class
5. Click **Get flights**
6. Results now include the extra companion voucher fare seats in Business class

You can also use the voucher on Iberia or Aer Lingus flights — same process, both are bookable via ba.com.

### 8d. Standard seat allocation per BA flight

BA guarantees a minimum number of reward seats per flight, released 355 days before departure:

| Cabin | Seats released | Notes |
|-------|---------------|-------|
| World Traveller (Economy) | 8 | Released at 355 days |
| World Traveller Plus (Premium Economy) | 2 | Released at 355 days |
| Club World (Business) | 4 | Released at 355 days |
| **Companion Voucher Fare seats** | Extra (varies) | Only visible when voucher box ticked |

More seats (of all types) often open up as departure approaches. The 355-day release is most important for peak dates and popular routes (Cape Town, Maldives, Sydney, Caribbean over Christmas/summer).

### 8e. Booking rules

- **Eligible airlines**: BA, Iberia, Aer Lingus only. Cannot be used on Qatar Airways, Cathay Pacific, American Airlines, or any other oneworld partner.
- **You must be a named traveller** on the booking (you cannot gift the voucher).
- **Book AND fly the outbound** before the voucher's expiry date. The return can be at any time.
- **Maximum 2 vouchers** per booking (allows 4 people to travel 2-for-1).
- **Cancellations**: £35 per person fee; Avios and voucher returned to account (expiry date unchanged).
- **Name changes**: The companion's name cannot be changed after booking.

### 8f. Advance booking strategy (355 days)

For peak routes in Business class, seats sell out quickly at the 355-day mark. Tips:

- Book **outbound at 355 days out**: seats open at midnight GMT (1am BST in summer)
- You **cannot** book return flights at the same time (they're not on sale yet)
- For the return, wait until 355 days before the return date, then either:
  - Call BA's **call centre at midnight GMT** to add return flights to your existing booking (they can hold seats while completing the booking — unlike the website)
  - Or book online separately (full Avios for both passengers), then call BA to link and apply the voucher (gets refunded)
- The **website loses the race** at midnight: multiple people can have the same seats in their basket, but only one can pay. Call centre agents can hold seats during the booking. Use the call centre for critical bookings.

**BA UK call centre numbers:**
- Gold: 0800 123 111
- Silver: 0800 408 0 009
- Bronze / all others: 0800 597 7580

**For midnight GMT bookings**, UK call centres are closed. Use the **US call centre** instead (time zone aligns): +1-800-247-9297

### 8g. SeatSpy and third-party tools cannot see companion voucher seats

> ⚠️ SeatSpy, Reward Flight Finder, and Award Travel Finder **cannot detect** companion voucher fare (CVF) seats. They only show the standard reward seat pool.

This means if SeatSpy reports no Business class availability, it may still exist for companion voucher holders. **You must check directly on ba.com with the voucher box ticked to see the full picture.** There is no workaround — these extra seats are only surfaced through BA's own logged-in booking system.

### 8h. Maximising voucher value

- Use it on **long-haul Business or First** — the more Avios the companion seat would cost, the more the voucher saves you
- **LHR–JFK return, Club World, off-peak**: 100,000 Avios (50,000 pp) for two people with voucher — saving 50,000 Avios vs two separate bookings
- **LHR–BGI or LHR–Caribbean, Club World**: 125,000 Avios for a couple return — strong value
- **Avoid using the voucher on Economy** unless you hold the free BA Amex (which restricts it to Economy anyway) — you leave enormous value on the table
- The 2-year validity gives time to plan

### 8i. Upgrading with Avios (separate from Reward Flights)

Avios can also be used to upgrade a *paid* cash ticket to the next cabin on eligible BA flights. The Avios cost depends on route distance and fare class. Upgrade inventory typically opens 24–48 hours before departure and is not guaranteed at booking. For most people, a Reward Flight booking (especially with the Companion Voucher) offers better predictability and value than upgrading a cash ticket.

Details: [BA Upgrade with Avios](https://www.britishairways.com/en-gb/information/avios-programmes/british-airways-executive-club/spending-avios/upgrade-with-avios)

## 9. Economy Class Reward Flights

Most guides focus on premium cabins, but Economy ("World Traveller") Avios redemptions are worth knowing about.

### When Economy redemptions make sense

**Short-haul Europe (Zones 1–3)**: The best use of Economy Avios. At 4,750–10,750 Avios one-way for destinations across Europe, with low carrier charges, these are genuinely competitive against cash. Particularly useful for:
- Last-minute short breaks where cash prices spike
- Business travel to European cities
- Filling a gap between earning Avios and having enough for Business class

| Zone | Route example | Economy off-peak | Economy peak | Approx cash taxes |
|------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|-------------------|
| Z1 | LHR–EDI, LHR–AMS | 4,750 | 5,250 | ~£30–50 |
| Z2 | LHR–BCN, LHR–HEL | 7,250 | 8,250 | ~£50–80 |
| Z3 | LHR–ATH, LHR–IST | 9,250 | 10,750 | ~£80–120 |

**Long-haul Economy**: More nuanced. The Avios cost is lower than Business, but BA still levies carrier surcharges in Economy — lower in absolute terms but the same in principle. At Zone 5 (e.g. New York) the off-peak Economy price is 13,000 Avios one-way. With ~£100–150 in taxes/surcharges, you're paying around £230–280 total for a one-way Economy seat to New York. On cheap fares that's poor value; during peak periods when cash fares spike, it becomes attractive.

| Zone | Route example | Economy off-peak | Economy peak |
|------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Z5 | LHR–JFK, LHR–ORD | 13,000 | 20,000 |
| Z6 | LHR–MIA, LHR–LAX, LHR–BGI | 16,250 | 25,000 |
| Z7 | LHR–NRT, LHR–GRU | 19,500 | 30,000 |
| Z9 | Long-haul partner routes | 32,500–51,500 | — |

### Economy vs Business: the value question

Using Avios for Economy on long-haul BA flights is generally **not the best use** of points. The key comparison:

- **Economy to New York**: 13,000 Avios + ~£130 taxes one-way ≈ total "cost" of £260 (at 1p/Avios)
- **Cash Economy to New York**: Often £250–500 one-way
- **Business to New York**: 50,000 Avios + ~£250 cash one-way. Cash equivalent ~£750+ one-way. Much better value per Avios.

The sweet spot is short-haul Economy (Zones 1–3) and long-haul Business — Economy Avios on transatlantic routes sits in a middle ground that rarely wins against either cash tickets or saving for Business.

### Economy and the Companion Voucher

The Premium Plus Companion Voucher works in Economy as well as Business. However, for two Economy tickets to New York, a Companion Voucher saves you 13,000 Avios (off-peak). That same voucher saves you 50,000 Avios on Business class. The maths strongly favours using the voucher for the most expensive cabin you'd realistically fly.

### Economy availability

8 Economy reward seats are typically available per BA flight (vs 4 Club World). This means Economy availability is generally easier to find and less time-critical at the 355-day mark.

```
https://www.britishairways.com/travel/redeem/execclub/_gf/en_gb?tab_selected=redeem&redemption_type=STD_RED
```

The `tab_selected=redeem&redemption_type=STD_RED` parameters select the Reward Flight tab by default. This is the only parameter worth using — it saves one click. Everything else must be entered manually.

**What it shows:** All Reward Flight options — BA, IB, EI own flights plus oneworld partner availability.

**Class dropdown values** (verified June 2026):

| Option shown | Internal value |
|-------------|----------------|
| Economy | `M` |
| Premium economy | `W` |
| Business/Club | `C` |
| First | `F` |

**After clicking "Get flights":** The system processes the search and redirects to a results page at:

```
https://www.britishairways.com/travel/redeem/execclub/_gf/en_gb
```

**No parameters appear in the results URL** — all search state is stored server-side. The results page is not bookmarkable and cannot be shared or reconstructed. This was verified live in June 2026: submitting a LHR→JFK, Business class, 2 adults, 15 Oct 2026 search produced a results page at the bare URL above with no query string.

> ⚠️ **No constructable or bookmarkable results URL exists — verified June 2026.** Unlike the Qantas Flight Reward Finder (which supports deep-link parameters), BA's booking search has no equivalent. Do not spend time trying to reverse-engineer URL parameters for results — none exist. Every session must start from the search form.

**Companion voucher checkbox:** When logged in with an active voucher, a "Use a Companion Voucher" checkbox appears at the top of the form. **This must be ticked before searching** — it is not a checkout-only step. Ticking it reveals additional Club World seats in results. See [Section 8](#8-companion-voucher--complete-guide) for full details.

**Results page observations** (verified June 2026, LHR→JFK, Business class):
- Results show outbound flights in a table with columns: Premium Economy / Business Class / First (regardless of the class selected in the search form — all cabins appear)
- Off-peak dates are labelled clearly
- Seat count shown when limited (e.g. "7 left", "4 left")
- A date strip above the results lets you navigate to nearby dates
- After selecting outbound, the inbound section appears below on the same page
- A "Journey summary" panel at the bottom tracks selections and shows the Continue button
- The Continue button remains greyed out until both outbound and inbound are selected

## 11. Route Tips by Destination

### For North America
- Assess BA vs AA surcharges before booking. On a Business class round-trip, you may pay £300–500 less in cash by booking AA (at a slightly higher Avios cost on the single-partner chart).
- The Iberia Plus transfer + MAD booking strategy is powerful for transatlantic routes if you're flexible about connecting via Madrid.
- Use the Companion Voucher for this region — 100,000 Avios (50,000 × 2) for two Business seats to New York off-peak is excellent value.

### For the Caribbean
- BA has strong direct connectivity from LHR (Barbados, Nassau, Montego Bay, Antigua, Tobago).
- Zone 6 at 62,500 Avios Business off-peak is a strong sweet spot, particularly with the Companion Voucher.
- Surcharges are meaningful but the route convenience is high.

### For Finland
- Helsinki is Zone 2 — 13,500 Avios Business off-peak is outstanding value for a ~2.5 hr flight.
- Finnair's Business (on wide-body routes) and their new short-haul Business product are both solid.
- Helsinki can also serve as a gateway to Asia on Finnair's medium-haul connections to Bangkok, Seoul, and Tokyo (though these would be separate Avios bookings).

### For Japan and Korea
- BA flies LHR–NRT and LHR–HND direct. The World Traveller Plus (Premium Economy) and Club World products are strong.
- Zone 7 at 75,000 off-peak is reasonable value for ~12 hr flights.
- If BA surcharges are prohibitive, JAL (via ba.com or as a single partner) offers an alternative with moderate fees and an excellent Business product (JAL Seasons).
- Avoid peak pricing to Japan (Golden Week, August, New Year) — the 90,000 peak rate adds 15,000 Avios vs off-peak.

### For South America
- LHR–GRU (São Paulo) is BA's main South American destination. Zone 7 at 75,000 off-peak.
- BA's Club Suite (Club World) is the best product for this route, introduced on the route in 2023.

### For Australia
- This is the most complex redemption from London. The key points:
  - BA doesn't fly to Australia; partner bookings are required.
  - Zone 9 single-partner (154,500 Business) is the most Avios-efficient route.
  - Partner availability on Qantas, Cathay Pacific, and Malaysia Airlines can be patchy; search well in advance.
  - The Qantas FF programme (MEL/SYD origin) is usually better value for return legs from Australia — consider booking one-way in each direction through different programmes.
